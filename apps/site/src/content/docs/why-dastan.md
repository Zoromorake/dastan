## Local-first

Your screenplay lives in **your browser's IndexedDB** on your device. You can write offline. Dastan does not need a server to store your draft.

**Why:** scripts are intellectual property. They should not sit on a stranger's disk by default.

## Open source & AGPL

Dastan is licensed under **AGPL-3.0**. The code is public and auditable.

**What AGPL means for you as a writer:** nothing changes about your scripts. You own your writing. AGPL constrains people who **redistribute or modify the software**, not people who **use** it to write.

If you only run Dastan for yourself, AGPL does not claim your screenplays.

## AI on your terms

Bring your own API key. Requests go to **your** provider. Editor-mode edits require **accept or reject**. Dastan does not train models on your pages.

**Why:** "AI wrote my script" is not the product. Assisted, reviewable drafting is.

## Business model

> **Writing is free.** AI compute, convenience, collaboration, and enterprise support fund the project.

Expect paid tiers later for managed AI, cloud sync, and collaboration — not for basic local writing.

## What we deliberately have not faked

| Feature | Status |
|---------|--------|
| True WYSIWYG pagination in the editor | Not yet — preview only |
| Page locking / colored production pages | Not yet — needs true pagination |
| Real-time collaboration | Scaffolded; listed as coming soon |
| Managed Dastan Cloud AI | Separate private repo / future |

We would rather be honest than show fake page breaks that mislead you on set.

## Comparison mindset

Dastan optimizes for **ownership** (local files, open source, BYOK AI) over **all-in-one production lock-in**. Final Draft's decades of production pagination pedigree remain the bar for on-set PDF fidelity — Dastan's export path is strong and improving; live pagination is not equivalent yet.
